Amorak — Sound Design

World-building through immersive audio design and character sound signatures.

Audio in Context

Watch how sound design builds the horror atmosphere. Every creature growl, environmental layer, and spatial detail was crafted to create immersive tension.

Audio-Driven Storytelling

Amorak's world is built through sound. Character signatures, environmental layers, and spatial audio create atmospheric horror that scales with camera proximity, keeping the mix readable while reinforcing the story's dark tone.

Creature Audio Design

Each creature has a unique sonic identity built from organic textures, processed animal vocalizations, and synthesized sub-frequencies. The beast's presence is felt through low-frequency rumbles that scale with proximity, creating an unsettling atmosphere even when off-screen.

Sub-Bass Foundation

40-200Hz rumbles provide body and presence, felt more than heard, creating visceral unease

Organic Texture Layers

Processed animal vocalizations create believable yet otherworldly creature sounds

Proximity-Based Mix

Audio intensity and detail scale with camera distance for dynamic immersion

Environmental Sound Design

The basement environment uses layered ambient textures - dripping water, creaking wood, distant echoes, and subtle electrical hums. These elements dynamically respond to the camera position, creating depth and spatial awareness.

Layered Ambience

Dripping water, creaking wood, and electrical hums build oppressive atmosphere

Spatial Audio Design

Elements positioned in 3D space guide viewer attention and create depth

Dynamic Response

Soundscapes adapt to camera movement for seamless immersion

Technical Audio Implementation

Frequency Architecture

Creature sounds occupy 40-200Hz for body and presence. Environmental details fill mid-range. High frequencies reserved for tension-building textures. Strategic allocation ensures clarity and impact.

Progressive Layering

Audio layers build throughout scenes, starting minimal and adding complexity as tension increases. Maintains engagement without overwhelming the mix during critical moments.

3D Spatial Mixing

Sound elements adjust based on camera distance and angle. Close-ups emphasize intimate details; wide shots focus on ambience. Creates seamless immersive experience.
