Kierunek Audio

Audio projektowałem pod czytelność i energię wyścigu: wyraźna telemetria zdarzeń, dynamiczna publiczność i UI, które nie zagłusza gry. Brzmienie celowo „arcade”, ale z kontrolą transjentów i selektywnym nasyceniem, by utrzymać gęsty miks w 1–4 graczach.

  • Warstwy: gameplay > czytelność UI > crowd/ambience
  • Wskaźniki stanów (start, bursty, hazardy, finisz)
  • Ograniczenie maskowania widmem i priorytety zdarzeń

MetaSounds

Cały system audio zbudowany w MetaSounds – nowoczesnym systemie proceduralno-parametrycznym w Unreal Engine. Pozwala na reaktywny, modularny sound design reagujący na gameplay w czasie rzeczywistym.

  • Proceduralne warstwy zależne od prędkości i stanu gry
  • Parametryczne kontrolery dla pitch/filtrów/modulacji
  • Modularne patche umożliwiające szybkie iteracje

Rola — Audio

Odpowiadałem za sound effects, implementację i miks: paleta SFX gameplayu, UI, warstwy crowd/ambience oraz integrację i miks w silniku dla czytelności przy szybkiej akcji. System reaguje na stany wyścigu i zdarzenia gracza.

  • Responsywne SFX powiązane z akcjami gracza i przeszkodami
  • Hierarchia miksu zoptymalizowana pod czytelność (1–4 graczy)
  • Niewchodzące w drogę warstwy dynamiczne przy intensywnych momentach

SFX System

A parametric library: speed, track surface and cooldown windows drive layers and transients. Boost/burst has its own RPM curves and noise sweetener. Collisions use weights and thresholds to avoid spam.

  • Track materials (asphalt/sand/metal) with smooth crossfades
  • Hazards/slip – pitch/LPF dependent on camera and speed
  • Countdown/Start and Finish stingers as round anchors

Mix & Dynamics

Priority‑based mix with ducking: callouts and UI sit above the bed while the crowd 'breathes' with race momentum. Submix processing reins in peaks and keeps loudness consistent during chaos.

  • Side‑chain ducking for UI/callouts over gameplay
  • Automatic crowd/ambience rides from state and position
  • Gain staging and reduced spectral masking

Unreal Integration

Sound Cues with parameters and Submix buses. Concurrency prevents event pile‑ups; attenuation profiles shape distance; light bus limiting stabilizes perceived loudness at scale.

  • Race state parameters + speed/camera inputs
  • Submixes: UI, Gameplay, Ambience + limiter on Master
  • Concurrency and collision trigger thresholds

Technical Metrics

Comprehensive breakdown of audio implementation scope, performance metrics, and resource management.

  • 320+ audio events — Across 12 categories: player actions, environment, UI, crowd, hazards, boost, collision, surface transitions, state changes, countdowns, finishes, and victory stingers
  • 180 MB audio footprint — Optimized with streaming and compression (Vorbis @ variable quality). ~450 source files processed to final assets
  • Max 32 concurrent voices — Priority-based voice culling: UI/gameplay critical (priority 1.0) > player SFX (0.8) > crowd/ambience (0.5). Automatic stealing on overflow
  • < 45ms input-to-audio — Maintained across all gameplay scenarios with real-time parameter modulation and MetaSounds processing
  • ~85 MB peak RAM usage — Includes loaded banks, streaming buffer, and active DSP. Managed via Unreal's audio streaming system

Challenges & Solutions

Real-world problems encountered during development and the technical solutions implemented.

⚠️

4-Player Mix Clarity

Problem:

With up to 4 simultaneous players, collision and boost sounds would stack and cause frequency masking, making UI feedback inaudible.

Solution:

Implemented priority-based concurrency limiting (max 4 collision sounds at once) with intelligent voice stealing. Added frequency-conscious EQ curves per player slot (slot 1: 200-800Hz focus, slot 2: 800-2kHz, etc.) and applied sidechain ducking on crowd/ambience when UI events trigger (-6dB duck, 50ms attack, 200ms release).

🔊

Surface Transition Pops

Problem:

Players moving between track materials (asphalt → sand → metal) caused audible pops due to instant layer switching.

Solution:

Replaced instant switches with crossfade system using Unreal's blend containers. Added 150ms crossfade time with equal-power curve. Surface detection uses overlap sphere with 0.2s cooldown to prevent rapid transitions.

💾

Boost Sound Memory Spikes

Problem:

Original boost SFX used 4-layer system with 24-bit WAV files, causing 40MB memory spikes per instance (x4 players = potential 160MB spike).

Solution:

Refactored to procedural MetaSounds patch: noise generator + band-pass filter sweep + pitch modulation driven by boost intensity parameter. Reduced to <2MB per instance while maintaining sonic character. Added RPM-style pitch curve for organic acceleration feel.
