    Richter — Sound Design

    Complete soundscape designed with original recordings and synthesis for short film.

    Project Showcase

    Project Overview

    For my DAW 2 exam assignment, I created the complete soundscape for the short film 'Richter'. The focus was on original sound design without relying on existing sound libraries. I combined field recordings, synthesizer-based design, and creative layering to build a dynamic and immersive audio world.

    Field Recording

    Captured unique ambiences and environmental textures using Zoom H6 recorder while walking through the city.

    Sound Synthesis

    Used Vital, ReaSynth, and TAL Noisemaker to create drones, risers, and alarms.

    Sound Design

    Designed vehicle engines, earthquake rumbles, whooshes, and environmental effects.

    Mixing & Processing

    Applied EQ, distortion, pitch shifting, and spatial effects with parameter automation.

    Technical Approach

    Each sound was carefully layered, processed, and mixed with attention to detail. I automated parameters such as volume, panning, and reverb sends to enhance realism and movement, including recreating the Doppler effect. The project used a 48kHz/24-bit setup with loudness metering to maintain professional standards.
